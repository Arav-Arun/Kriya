# Policy: Rewards

- Policy ID: POL-005
- Version: 1.6 | Effective: 01-Apr-2026
- Owner: Customer Service

## Description

Covers reward point accrual issues (missing or short-credited points), redemption failures, point expiry queries, and promotional bonus point disputes. Standard earn rate is 2 points per ₹100 retail spend; accelerated categories (dining, travel) earn 5 points per ₹100 during active promotions.

## Eligibility Rules

1. Points accrue within 7 days of the statement date; accrual complaints are valid only after this window passes.
2. Excluded spend categories earn no points: fuel, rent payments, wallet loads, EMI principal, cash advances, government payments, and insurance premiums above ₹50,000 per cycle.
3. Redemption requires a minimum balance of 500 points; redemption value is ₹0.25 per point against statement credit, higher for catalogue/travel redemptions.
4. Points expire 24 months after accrual, on a first-in-first-out basis. Expired points are not reinstated except for documented system errors.
5. Promotional bonus points follow the specific campaign terms, including minimum-spend and cap rules.

## Required Documents

- Transaction details for the disputed accrual (date, merchant, amount)
- Campaign name or offer communication, for promotional point disputes

## Assigned Team

Customer Service

## SLA

- First response: within 24 hours
- Point adjustment (where approved): within 7 working days

## Escalation Conditions

- Disputed points exceed 50,000 → Customer Service supervisor approval required for adjustment.
- Systemic accrual error affecting multiple customers (same merchant/MCC misclassification) → Card Operations technical desk for batch correction.
- Suspected reward abuse (split transactions, merchant collusion, self-swipe) → Risk Operations.

## Resolution Procedure

1. Verify the transaction posted and identify its merchant category code (MCC).
2. Check the MCC against the exclusion list and any active campaign rules.
3. Recalculate expected points; compare with credited points.
4. If short-credited, post the adjustment with a customer notification.
5. For redemption failures, check point balance, expiry, and catalogue availability; re-process or refund points.
6. Close with the adjustment reference and an explanation of the applicable earn rule.
