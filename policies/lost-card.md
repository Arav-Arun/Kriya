# Policy: Lost Card

- Policy ID: POL-007
- Version: 1.9 | Effective: 01-Apr-2026
- Owner: Card Operations

## Description

Covers reporting, hotlisting (permanent block), and replacement of lost or stolen credit cards, including liability protection for the period after the loss is reported. Hotlisting is irreversible; customers unsure whether the card is misplaced may use a temporary freeze instead.

## Eligibility Rules

1. Any cardholder (primary or add-on) may report a card lost; add-on card hotlisting may be requested by the primary cardholder.
2. Zero customer liability for all transactions after the report timestamp.
3. Transactions between the actual loss and the report are assessed under the RBI limited-liability matrix (see POL-002).
4. Replacement is issued to the registered mailing address only; address changes within the previous 30 days require KYC re-verification before dispatch.
5. Replacement fee ₹250 plus GST, waived for premium card variants and for theft cases with an FIR.

## Required Documents

- None for hotlisting (identity verification via registered mobile + security questions or app login)
- FIR / e-FIR copy if the card was stolen and unauthorized transactions occurred

## Assigned Team

Card Operations

## SLA

- Hotlisting: immediate, within 30 minutes of report
- Replacement card dispatch: within 3 working days
- Delivery: within 7 working days (metro), 10 working days (non-metro)

## Escalation Conditions

- Unauthorized transactions found between loss and report → register a parallel fraud case with Fraud Operations under POL-002.
- Third card replacement within 12 months → Risk Operations review for account compromise or misuse pattern.
- Card reported lost while an international trip is ongoing → emergency card / cash assistance desk, expedited courier at bank cost.

## Resolution Procedure

1. Verify the caller's identity; hotlist the card immediately and confirm via SMS/email with timestamp.
2. Review the last 72 hours of transactions with the customer; flag any unauthorized ones to Fraud Operations.
3. Disable all digital tokens (wallets, tokenized merchants) linked to the card number.
4. Initiate replacement with a new card number; carry over limit, EMIs, and reward points.
5. Confirm dispatch tracking details to the customer.
6. Close with hotlist timestamp and replacement reference recorded.
